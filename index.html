Nice to Meet you,

I'm Andrea Risjord, a UX Researcher and Designer. I enjoy understanding human stores through thorough and empathetic research and applying my skills to solve human problems with functional and intuitive designs.

I always thought I would be an Anthropological researcher, but I had a hard time envisioning myself in that world. After graduating with a Bachelor of Arts in Visual Arts and Anthropology from Emory University, I fell into the world of art and manufacturing. That experience allowed me time working in high-pressure team environments, juggling client expectations, and managing workflow between complex and independent teams. However, I continued to search for the missing link. I was striving for an outlet for my constant questions about life as a human being and how we experience this world.

In 2022 I was introduced to UX. I had no idea what it was, but the more I learned, the more I felt like the pieces were finally fitting together for me. UX research is the perfect combination of research meets design that I'd been looking for. I decided to pursue a post-graduate certificate in UX/UI Research and design from UMN which will finish in May of 2023.

Now, I specialize in problem-finding and solving using functional and beautiful design. Research is where it all started for me and that is still where I find joy. Understanding people's stories and communicating their experiences gives me a feeling of connection to my community and to humankind as a whole while discovering ways to solve problems scratches my itch for efficiency and order.

My design aesthetic is influenced by my studies in art history and anthropology, my musical taste, and various hobbies.
